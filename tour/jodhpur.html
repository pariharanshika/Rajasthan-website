JODHPUR

Mehrangarh Fort

Located in Jodhpur, Mehrangarh Fort is one of the largest forts in the country.
It is located at the top of a 410 feet elevated hill and guarded by huge walls.

Umaid Bhawan Palace

Umaid Bhawan Palace is also a major tourist attraction in Jodhpur.
In 1944, it was the last of the greatest palaces built in Jodhpur.
It is named after the then Maharaja of Jodhpur, Raja Umaid Singh.

Toor_Ji_Ka_Baori

Toorji Ka Jhalra, commonly called stepwell of Jodhpur,
is an intricate design of stepwell, one of the few remaining structures depicting the traditional water management systems of Jodhpur.

Rai Ka Baag Palace

Constructed in 1663 by the queen of Maharaja Jaswant Singh- I, Hadiji, Rai ka Baag Palace is one of the popular tourist attractions of Jodhpur.
The palace is octagonal in shape and currently it houses the income tax office of Jodhpur

Sheesh Mahal

The Sheesh Mahal in the Mehrangarh Fort of Jodhpur, known as the Glass Palace of Jodhpur, is a marvellous piece of historic architecture is decked from ceiling to floor with fanciful mirror work.
It is said to be the bedchamber of Maharaja Ajit Singh between the 17th and 18th centuries.

Mahamandir

Located two kilometres away from Jodhpur on the road towards Mandore, it is a very big temple with he fantastically carved 84 pillars which are a sight to behold.

Khejarla Fort

The Khejarla Fort is an experience worth trying out.
Originally built in the 17th century by the Maharaja of Jodhpur, the 400-year old edifice has since been turned into a hotel,
with amenities that exhibit contemporary luxury laid out against the backdrop of the royal fortress.

Mandore Garden

Mandore Gardens is located about 10 kilometres from the city of Jodhpur.
The garden contains cenotaphs which are built like traditional Hindu temples unlike other cenotaphs.
The garden also contains a ruined fort and palace.

Kaylana Lake

Kaylana Lake is a massive manmade lake on the outskirts of Jodhpur city.
It serves as the primary resource for drinking water for the citizens of Jodhpur and other neighbouring settlements.
It also serves as an excellent viewpoint for sunsets.

Ghanta Ghar

The Ghanta Ghar in Jodhpur is a magnificent clock tower in the centre of the city which offers a
brilliant panoramic view of the city from the top, surrounded by the busiest and largest marketplace of Jodhpur.
